Coding

I was first introduced to web development / coding years ago. Originally, I was trying to develop a website to host my guitar videos. This site would allow users to download tabs and other things related to the songs. I never actually finished it, but it allowed me to get a taste of it.

Later on a friend of mine recommended me to learn C# to learn app development. I later switched to JavaScript, but this was the first programming language I actually learned. I wanted to create an exercise app that would allow me to automate a lot of the tracking that I currently do. I had trouble creating a visual product so that lead me back to HTML, CSS, & JavaScript.

After going through a couple of jobs and realizing how much I enjoyed programming, I knew I needed to make a shift. There's something to love about the process of beating your head against a wall, but perservering through that adversity. Once you finally succeed the feeling is incredible.

Powerlifting
